SFO Logo

SFO's logo is the main, corporate face of the brand. The SFO Logo should be used in all consumer-facing and internal corporate communications including, but not limited to:

• Website
• Wayfinding Graphics
• Airport Signage
• Uniforms
• Advertising
• Social Media
• Promotions
• Communications

As such, we have developed guidelines and standards for its consistent usage in a variety of situations and media. In general, the preferred version for use in the majority of situations is the color version on a white background. In cases where the logo must appear on a dark background the secondary reversed logo should be used.

If you need clarification on logo usage, or have any questions about which logo to use where, please contact Sara Magoffin in Communication & Marketing.



SFO Dimensional Logo

A dimensional version of the logo has been created for use in on-screen display applications.

SFO Dimensional Signature

This signature combines the SFO logo and San Francisco International Airport logotype. It is available in both horizontal and vertical configurations.

This version is appropriate for use with general or international audiences who may not be familiar with the "SFO" airport code.

SFO Flat Logo

A flat, one-color version of the SFO logo has been created for use in print applications as well as screen applications where the dimensional logo is problematic.

SFO Logotype

The SFO logotype uses the Myriad typeface, an elegant, sans serif font, in a specially designed version of the words San Francisco International Airport.

SFO Signature, Primary

The signature combines the SFO logo and SFO logotype as shown below.

SFO Signature, Secondary

A secondary signature is available in both a horizontal and vertical configuration. It is provided for use in applications where the primary signature is not accommodated due to space or format restrictions.

Color

Two-color SFO Signature

The PANTONE colors for the SFO signature are PANTONE 308 for the SFO logo and PANTONE Cool Gray 11 for the SFO logotype. "SFO" and flight lines always appear in white.

One-color SFO Signature

The signature appears in either PANTONE Cool Gray 11 or Black. "SFO" and flight lines always appear in white.

Reversed SFO Signature

The signature can only appear reversed on a black, dark gray, PANTONE Cool Gray 11, or PANTONE 308 background. The letters "SFO" and the flight lines always appear in the background color.

Usage

Print Usage for SFO Logo, SFO Logotype and SFO Signature

To establish and maintain a world class brand, it is important that our identity appears consistent across all our communications. Therefore, it is critical to never alter the logo, logotype or signature, this includes adding or deleting elements or using unauthorized colors. Under certain circumstances, for example if space is limited, the SFO logo or a specially configured SFO signature may be used. Please review these guidelines for proper use of the SFO logo, SFO logotype and SFO signature.

SFO Signature, Primary

The primary use of the SFO signature appears as a bleed off the top right side of the page, as shown here. It should be used on all communications and items unless it conflicts with any formatting restrictions.

Clear Space

Keep the area around the signature clear of distracting or conflicting elements, such as textured backgrounds and illustrations. When using the signature on either a bright or dark background or photograph, make sure there is sufficient contrast. The minimum clear space required is .5X, where X equals the height of the left side of the logo.

Minimum Size

Specially designed versions of the SFO signature and SFO logo are available at very small sizes. Note that the flight lines in the logo have been widened to prevent fill-in. Do not use the standard SFO signature for any sizes smaller than 1.0".


SFO Signature 1.25 version, .75 and .5 Versions

Use for sizes between 1.25" and 1".
(The signature is not used smaller than 1".)

Unacceptable Usage for SFO Logo, SFO Logotype and SFO Signature

Do not transpose the color of the SFO logo
and the SFO logotype.

Do not use insufficient contrast between
the SFO signature and background.

Do not apply a three-dimensional effect to
the SFO signature.

Do not use any other color than those
specified in the SFO signature section.

Do not use the SFO logotype alone.

Do not allow the background color to fill
the letters SFO and the flight lines.

Do not cut off the SFO logo when it bleeds
to the top of the page.

Do not extend the color background of the
SFO logo when bleeding off the top of the page.
